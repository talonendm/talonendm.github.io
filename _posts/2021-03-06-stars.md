# Stars
The Big Dipper, or should I say [Plough](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Dipper) or Otava is well known constellation. A group of 7 stars forming an easily recognizable pattern. In this post only few of eighty-eight (88) constellations with defined boundaries are described.

- Big Dipper: see huge image [here](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/c4/Big_dipper_from_the_kalalau_lookout_at_the_kokee_state_park_in_hawaii.jpg).
- Orion, 3 stars in the line; when: [270#8451; 2/2021 20:00].  Orion's seven brightest stars form a distinctive [hourglass-shaped](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orion_(constellation)) asterism. See huge image [here](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4d/Orion_3008_huge.jpg).
- Auriga (fi Ajomies).
